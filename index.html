Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income


The above chart displays data collected in 2014 by the American Community Survey (ASC), and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS). The most visible trends are positive correlations, between the risk factors (obesity, smoking, and lack of healthcare coverage) and poverty levels. States with a lower median household income, and higher poverty level rates show higher obesity and smoking rates, and a lower percentage of healthcare coverage.

At a quick glance, the median age in most US states is between 36-40 years old. We can see in the scatter plots for this age group the lack of healthcare coverage rates clusters between 8-16%, smoking rates clusters between 14-20%, and obesity rates are a bit higher clustering between 26-32%.
